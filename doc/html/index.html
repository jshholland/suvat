Suvat, the motion simulator

This is the documentation for Suvat version 0.2. Suvat is a small application that uses the laws of motion under constant acceleration to calculate details of the motion of an object.

But what does this actually mean? What you see when you run Suvat is a window with 5 labelled entry boxes and two buttons. After a small amount of experimentation, you will find that you are expected to enter three numbers and press the "Calculate" button. If the numbers make sense in a basic physical sense, Suvat will calculate the remaining details of motion and fill in the remaining entry boxes.

An example might help make more sense. Say you enter the numbers as follows:

  • 10 in "Displacement"
  • 3 in "Original velocity"
  • 7 in "Time"
Pressing calculate will then fill in the remaining boxes:
  • -0.142857142857 in "Final velocity"
  • -0.448979591837 in "Acceleration"
These are the correct details for an object to travel 10 metres in 7 seconds starting with a velocity of 3 metres per second.
